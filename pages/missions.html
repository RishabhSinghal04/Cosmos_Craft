Space Missions Timeline

Sputnik 1 (1957)

The USSR launched the first artificial satellite, marking the start of the Space Race.

Yuri Gagarin (1961)

Gagarin became the first human to orbit Earth aboard Vostok 1.

Apollo 11 (1969)

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the Moon, a defining moment in human history.

Salyut 1 (1971)

The USSR launched the world's first space station.

Viking 1 (1976)

NASA's Viking 1 became the first successful Mars lander.

Space Shuttle Columbia (1981)

The first reusable spacecraft launched, beginning the Shuttle era.

Hubble Telescope (1990)

Launched into orbit, Hubble revolutionized astronomy with deep-space images.

International Space Station (1998-2000)

The first ISS module launched in 1998; continuously inhabited since 2000.

Curiosity Rover (2012)

NASA's rover landed on Mars and continues to explore the Martian surface.
